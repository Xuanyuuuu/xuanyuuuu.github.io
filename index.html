About me

Hi. I have a diverse academic and professional background that has shaped my passion for problem-solving and research. I graduated with a degree in biomedical engineering in 2018 and began my career as an embedded systems engineer in a small company, where I gained valuable insights into medical product development. Later, I served as a radiology technician in a state-owned hospital. Daily interactions with patients helped me realize how profoundly frontier research can benefit humanity.

Through these experiences, I became increasingly certain that I enjoy exploring problems and creating solutions. This led me to pursue graduate studies in neuroscience in France, an area that has captivated me the most. Thanks to this decision, I’ve had the opportunity to engage in exciting research projects and collaborate with a group of diligent and talented researchers, gradually becoming part of the academic community.

My ultimate interest is to explore the emergence of intelligence, a field I’d love to devote the next several years of my life to. As you can see in the Research section, my recent research experience has focused on this goal, leading me to explore some topics in cognition and deep learning. I’m currently looking for PhD opportunities in related topics, starting in November 2024.
