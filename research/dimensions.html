Probe Into the Mind: How the Narrative of a Movie Alters Similarity Judgements

Supervisor: Dr. Catherine Tallon-Baudry, Département d'Études Cognitives, École Normale Supérieure/PSL

Background

Hebart (2020) proposed a data-driven method to generate comprehensive interpretable dimensions regarding a collection of 1854 everyday-life objects. Their novel methodology uncovered all the dimensions we rely on for object recognition. Inspired by this work, we followed a similar pipeline, yet extended it from static natural objects to visual scenes from a movie. By establishing dimension spaces before and after watching the movie respectively, we aim to answer following questions.

a static image of fish

the mental representation of the fish in human brain

(Hebart, M.N., Zheng, C.Y., Pereira, F. et al. Revealing the multidimensional mental representations of natural objects underlying human similarity judgements. Nat Hum Behav 4, 1173–1185 (2020). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41562-020-00951-3)

Research Questions:

  1. What are the dimensions of mental representation of visual scences?
  2. Does narrative change the dimensions of categorization?
